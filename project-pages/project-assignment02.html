Storm Form
Printing cubes of fluid atmosphere.

In collaboration with Jimi Adeseun, MArch II 25'
Project Details
Atmospheric data is a good contender for modeling in three dimensions. However, how does swapping one physical dimension for the fourth dimension - time - change what we can learn from the weather? Storm form recorded the development and dispersion of multiple storms around the western hemisphere for physicalization in a hyper-dimensional, multi-material 3D print.
Developing weather patterns in Ecuador, Boston, Seattle, Chile, and in the Pacific Ocean were recorded by automating an export of NASA radar data from ArcGIS every 10 minutes. Then, voxel meshes were created in Grasshopper using VoxelTools and custom C# components to map radar image colors to mesh vertices. Finally, storm cubes for Ecuador and the Pacific were printed using Stratsys J55 multimaterial printer.
